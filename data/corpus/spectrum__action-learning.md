# Action Learning

Action learning (Revans, 1980; Pedler, 2011) employs a diverse group of learners in
 solving real-world, complex and urgent problems. Both individual learner development
 and team development is facilitated (Raelin, 1997) and a learner's cognitive development
 appears to be most enhanced the more difficult and complex the problem. In this problem-solving,
 authentic environment, the instructor takes the role of learning coach and facilitates
 an environment in which students become reflective practitioners (Marquardt, 2015).

> *We enjoy combining action learning and [action research](https://arnawebsite.org/) to design transdiciplinary courses that involve each student/student team proposing,
> performing and presenting original action research.*
