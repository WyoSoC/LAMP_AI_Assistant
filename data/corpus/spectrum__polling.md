# Polling

A quick classroom poll can allow students to self-assess their own understanding and
can allow the instructor and learning assistant to quickly assess class knowledge.
That is, polls are a great example of a Classroom Assessment Technique (CAT)! (Angelo
& Cross, 1993)

Polls can be accomplished with a variety of technologies, ranging from a use of fingers,
to paper to cell phones.

- Students can weigh in on a multiple choice question using one finger for A, two for
 B and so on. A single piece of paper can be divided into four quadrants and labeled
 A, B, C and D. [The CAPER Center for Astronomy and Physics](https://www.caperteam.com/classroom-resources) shows an example of an ABCD Card and other polling technologies.
- Cell phone polls can be accomplished using the Poll Everywhere software as well as
 many classroom response systems.
